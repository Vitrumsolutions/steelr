# Guest-Post Targets · Ranked

Sorted by realistic ROI: combination of Domain Authority, audience match, and editorial accessibility for a first-time contributor.

**Verify editor contact before each send.** Contacts listed here are the published editorial addresses at time of writing. Publications rotate editors. If an address bounces, check the masthead on the current issue or the website's "Contact" page.

---

## Tier 1 · High-authority, ideal audience

### 1. Homebuilding & Renovating
- **Site:** homebuilding.co.uk
- **Domain Authority:** ~68
- **Audience:** UK self-builders, major renovators, HNW homeowners. Perfect fit.
- **Why it matters:** Single highest-ROI target. They accept expert contributions from specialists and actively publish door/window content.
- **Submission route:** Pitch the Editorial Enquiries address at `editor@homebuilding.co.uk`. Published contributor form also at homebuilding.co.uk/contact.
- **Proposed angle:** "What UK homeowners get wrong when specifying a new front door" — expert perspective piece. Non-promotional. Positions SteelR implicitly through bylined author credit.
- **Use article:** `article-specifying-steel-front-doors.md`

### 2. Build It Magazine
- **Site:** self-build.co.uk
- **Domain Authority:** ~55
- **Audience:** UK self-builders, renovation projects, smaller scale than HBR.
- **Why it matters:** Actively seeks expert contributors for product guides. More accessible than HBR.
- **Submission route:** `editorial@self-build.co.uk`
- **Proposed angle:** Case study format. "How we specified a bespoke steel entrance for a Surrey new-build." With client permission, use a completed project.
- **Use article:** adapt `article-specifying-steel-front-doors.md` with case-study framing.

### 3. Grand Designs Magazine
- **Site:** granddesignsmagazine.com
- **Domain Authority:** ~55
- **Audience:** Aspirational, design-literate homeowners. Strong overlap with SteelR's premium positioning.
- **Why it matters:** Genuinely cares about design, not just product comparison. Loves architect+homeowner collaborations.
- **Submission route:** `editorial@granddesignsmagazine.com`
- **Proposed angle:** Opinion piece. "The return of the bespoke entrance: why homeowners are moving away from composite doors."
- **Use article:** adapted from `article-steel-doors-period-property.md`

### 4. Houzz UK (editorial)
- **Site:** houzz.co.uk
- **Domain Authority:** ~87 (Huge.)
- **Audience:** Affluent homeowners, interior designers, architects.
- **Why it matters:** Highest DA on this list. Regularly publishes "how to choose" content on doors and windows.
- **Submission route:** Houzz employs staff writers; pitch `editorial-uk@houzz.com` for a quote contribution (source for a piece) rather than a full byline. Still earns a link.
- **Proposed angle:** Quote contribution for any forthcoming "Front Door Ideas" or "Security for London Homes" article. Offer professional photography from lookbook.
- **Use article:** lookbook images + SteelR expert quotes.

### 5. Architects' Journal (AJ)
- **Site:** architectsjournal.co.uk
- **Domain Authority:** ~75
- **Audience:** UK architects and specifiers.
- **Why it matters:** Specifiers become multi-project clients. Even one architect reading this = meaningful long-term pipeline.
- **Submission route:** `news@architectsjournal.co.uk`; opinion/technical at `opinion@architectsjournal.co.uk`
- **Proposed angle:** Technical specification piece. "Specifying PAS 24 and SR3 residential entrance doors: a working guide for architects." Technical and valuable, not product-led.
- **Use article:** adapted from `article-sr3-vs-sr4-homeowner-guide.md` with technical-specifier framing.

## Tier 2 · Solid authority, good audience

### 6. The Construction Index
- **Site:** theconstructionindex.co.uk
- **Domain Authority:** ~60
- **Audience:** UK construction industry professionals.
- **Submission route:** `editor@theconstructionindex.co.uk`
- **Proposed angle:** Product news piece. "UK steel door manufacturer launches SR4 (LPS 1175) specification for residential." Straight news release format — easier editorial accept.

### 7. Period Living / Period Property UK
- **Site:** periodliving.co.uk (Future Publishing)
- **Domain Authority:** ~65
- **Audience:** Period property owners, conservation-area homeowners.
- **Submission route:** `periodliving@futurenet.com`
- **Proposed angle:** Conservation-appropriate steel doors for listed buildings. Hot niche, little competition.

### 8. Specify & Build
- **Site:** specifyandbuild.co.uk
- **Domain Authority:** ~40
- **Audience:** Specifiers, architects, commercial + residential.
- **Submission route:** website contact form or listed editor email.
- **Proposed angle:** Product showcase with technical detail. Easier editorial path; lower DA but still a useful citation.

### 9. Glass & Glazing Products / Glass Times
- **Site:** glassnews.co.uk and glasstimes.co.uk
- **Domain Authority:** ~50
- **Audience:** Glazing industry. Secondary audience, but SteelR is adjacent.
- **Submission route:** editor@glassnews.co.uk
- **Proposed angle:** Product news focusing on glazed-panel and sidelight options within steel door system.

### 10. FENSA / Secured by Design Member Content
- **Site:** fensa.org.uk, securedbydesign.com
- **Audience:** Already qualified leads searching for certified installers.
- **Route:** As a Secured by Design-approved supplier, you can submit case studies through the SBD members' portal. FENSA content is self-published via the membership portal.
- **Use:** Submit one SteelR install as a case study on each. Free to do. High-signal authority citation.

## Outreach priority

**Send these FIVE first, in this order:**

1. Homebuilding & Renovating (expert piece)
2. Architects' Journal (technical piece)
3. Houzz UK (quote contribution)
4. Build It (case study)
5. Grand Designs Magazine (opinion piece)

If any accept, place the pitch with tier 2. If all five say no, rework the pitch based on the first-round feedback before round 2.
