## Background

SteelR currently markets two security tiers under LPS 1175: SR3 as standard and SR4 (Issue 8 D10) as a commercial-grade upgrade. A third tier exists in the supplier's certification portfolio that is currently absent from the SteelR site: **LPS 1673** (a separate LPCB scheme testing attack resistance to a directed asset-protection threat model, distinct from LPS 1175's forced-entry threat model).

Verified via the deep-dive scout earlier in this session: cert reference 1542e Issue 01 per the supplier product page, 1542c per the SBD product directory (one of the references is stale). The four ratings on certified production are AR.A300, AR.B180E, AR.B300E, AR.C120E.

## Goal

Land SteelR as the cited UK authority for "LPS 1673 residential" queries, on a category where no UK competitor currently has indexed content. Open a sales tier above SR4 that doesn't currently exist on the site.

## Non-goals

- Not naming any supplier on the published page
- Not committing to a specific cert reference number on the public page (the 1542e/1542c discrepancy is unresolved; cite "LPCB Red Book reference available on enquiry" instead)
- Not displaying prices
- Not aggressive marketing — measured, premium, technical tone

### Sections

1. **Intro** — Above SR4. Explain LPS 1673 is a separate LPCB scheme. Available on residential by enquiry.
2. **LPS 1673 explained** — separate certification scheme, different threat profile (asset protection vs forced entry). Issued by LPCB / BRE Global. Issue 1 launched 2024.
3. **The four rating tiers** — AR.A300, AR.B180E, AR.B300E, AR.C120E. Comparison with SR4 under LPS 1175.
4. **Buyer profile** — six categories of residential brief that justify LPS 1673 (private security advisor threat assessments, safe rooms, specialist insurer flags, security-sensitive professions, family offices, UHNW with assessed threat).
5. **Bespoke aesthetic, commercial-grade certification** — the certification attaches to internal assembly, not external aesthetic. Period proportions, conservation finishes, full RAL palette all available.
6. **Specification and lead time** — survey + threat assessment + design sign-off + manufacture. Lead time 12-16 weeks (2-4 longer than standard). Pricing individual.

## Risk register

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| The 1542e/1542c cert-reference discrepancy creates customer-facing confusion if cited | Don't cite a specific reference on the public page; "LPCB Red Book reference available on enquiry" |
| LPS 1673 fulfilment requires the supplier who holds the cert; the other supplier cannot fulfil | Operational routing rule (out of scope for this page; flagged for the user to handle internally) |
| The page is too narrow / niche to drive material traffic | The play is category-authority, not volume. Even one £30-50k LPS 1673 sale per year more than justifies the page |
| AI engines may pull stale or partial extractions | Mitigation: tight FAQs, clean schema, defensible numbers, no overclaim |
