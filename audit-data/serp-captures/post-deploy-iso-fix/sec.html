The Four-Tier Residential Security Ladder

Standard, Enhanced, Commercial-grade, Ultra-high. Side by side.

BS EN 1627 (RC1 to RC6) and LPS 1175 (SR1 to SR8) are parallel certification schemes operating under different methodologies. They are not interchangeable. The LPCB has stated that over 90% of products tested under EN 1627 RC4 fail to achieve LPS 1175 SR2. SteelR ships RC4 as the standard residential specification on every door, with the LPCB-operated SR3, SR4 and LPS 1673 ratings as named upgrade tiers. The four tiers are not substitutes; they are layered options selected to match the brief.

Why new builds and flats require PAS 24, and why BS EN 1627 RC4 is what specifiers choose

Under Approved Document Q of the Building Regulations, all new-build homes and flats, including those created by material change of use, must be fitted with entrance doors that resist unauthorised access. PAS 24:2022 is the accepted standard to satisfy this requirement.

The Fire Safety Act 2021 further extended the Fire Safety Order to explicitly cover flat entrance doors in multi-occupied residential buildings. FD30S fire-rated doorsets are now the mandated minimum standard for any flat entrance door leading onto common parts.

Every SteelR door satisfies both requirements and exceeds both. BS EN 1627:2011 RC4 single leaf, unglazed certification means your door has been independently tested to withstand sustained forced entry with heavy-duty hand tools and battery-operated power tools, not just opportunist attack. It is the standard demanded by housing associations, developers and planning authorities on high-specification projects.
